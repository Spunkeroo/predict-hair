The Curly Girl Method Explained

The Curly Girl Method (CGM), popularized by Lorraine Massey's book, eliminates sulfates, silicones, heat, and harsh ingredients that damage curls. The core principles:

  • No sulfates: Use gentle cleansers or co-wash (conditioner-only washing) to preserve natural oils
  • No silicones: Silicones create buildup that blocks moisture. Use water-soluble alternatives
  • No heat styling: Air dry or diffuse on low heat only
  • No brushing dry hair: Detangle with fingers or wide-tooth comb on wet, conditioned hair only
  • Scrunch, don't rub: Scrunch water out with a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt — never rub with a terry cloth towel

Wash Day Routine

  1. Pre-poo (optional): Apply coconut oil or olive oil to dry hair 30 minutes before washing. Protects hair from hygral fatigue during washing
  2. Cleanse: Apply sulfate-free shampoo to scalp only. Massage with fingertips, let suds run down lengths. Rinse thoroughly
  3. Condition: Apply generous conditioner from mid-length to ends. Detangle with wide-tooth comb starting from ends, working up. Leave for 3-5 minutes. Squish to condish — cup sections and squeeze upward to encourage curl formation
  4. Style on soaking wet hair: Apply leave-in conditioner, then gel or mousse. Use praying hands method to distribute, then scrunch upward
  5. Dry: Plop in a microfiber towel for 15-20 minutes, then air dry or diffuse on low heat with a sock diffuser
  6. SOTC: Once 100% dry, scrunch out the crunch (SOTC) to break the gel cast. Fluff roots for volume

Styling Techniques

Plopping: Lay a microfiber towel flat, flip hair onto it, and wrap. This encourages curl formation and removes excess water without frizz. 15-20 minutes is ideal.

Diffusing: Use a diffuser attachment on low heat, low speed. Cup curls into the diffuser and bring to your head — don't hover and blast from above. Pixie diffusing (quick bursts of high heat) works for volume but can cause frizz.

Finger coiling: Wrap individual sections around your finger to define curls. Time-consuming but produces the most defined results for 3B-4C hair.

Pineapple method (sleeping): Gather all hair into a loose, high ponytail with a silk scrunchie before bed. Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase. This preserves curls for 2-3 days between wash days.

Common Mistakes

  • Touching hair while drying: The #1 cause of frizz. Once you've styled, don't touch until completely dry
  • Using too much product: Start with a quarter-sized amount and add more if needed. Excess product causes limp, greasy curls
  • Skipping deep conditioning: Deep condition every 1-2 weeks. This is non-negotiable for healthy curls
  • Brushing dry curly hair: This separates the curl clumps, creating frizz. Only detangle on wet, conditioned hair
  • Expecting overnight results: The CGM transition takes 2-8 weeks. Your hair needs time to recover from sulfates and heat damage

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I wash curly hair?

Most curly hair types do best with washing every 3-7 days. Wavy hair (Type 2) may need washing every 2-3 days, while coily hair (Type 4) can go 7-10 days between washes.

Can I start the Curly Girl Method with a regular shampoo?

You should do one final wash with a sulfate shampoo to remove all silicone buildup, then switch to sulfate-free products going forward. This reset wash is essential for CGM to work.

Why are my curls frizzy after following CGM?

Common causes: touching hair while drying, not using enough gel, using a terry cloth towel, or not applying products to wet enough hair. Apply stylers to soaking wet hair for best results.

Is the Curly Girl Method good for wavy hair?

Yes, but wavies often need modifications. Use lighter products, wash more frequently, and you may still need a gentle sulfate shampoo occasionally to prevent buildup that weighs waves down.

How long does the CGM transition take?

Typically 2-8 weeks. During this time, hair may look worse before it looks better as your scalp adjusts to less stripping and your curls recover their natural pattern.
